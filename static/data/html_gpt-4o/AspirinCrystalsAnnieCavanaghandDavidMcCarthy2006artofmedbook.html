Aspirin Crystals by Annie Cavanagh and David Mc Carthy (2006)

[a] Artist/ Group/Tribe

Annie Cavanagh and David Mc Carthy, renowned for capturing the unseen beauty of science through their microscopy art, collaborated on the visualization of aspirin crystals. Their joint efforts in revealing the aesthetic allure of scientific phenomena aim to bridge the gap between art and science, leading the viewer into a realm of microscopic beauty.

[b] Historical and Socio-Cultural Context (time-frame)

The image was created in 2006, a period characterized by a rapidly growing interest in interdisciplinary fields. It was a time when technology and art increasingly intersected, allowing artists like Cavanagh and Mc Carthy to delve into scientific subjects and present them in new, visually captivating ways.

[c] Symbolism and/ or Iconography

The artwork symbolizes the hidden beauty present within the ordinary. Aspirin, a commonly used medication, is shown in a light that transforms the mundane into the extraordinary, suggesting themes of transformation and the multifaceted nature of reality.

[d] Stylistic Significance (elements of art/ principles of design)

The image employs elements such as line, shape, and color to achieve a dynamic composition. The intricate crystal formations form sweeping lines and shadows, while the iridescent colors create a sense of depth and movement. These elements work together to emphasize the intricacy and complexity of microscopic structures.

[e] Social / Cultural Inequities

The accessibility and distribution of medications like aspirin are not uniform across societies, which are influenced by economic and social factors. The artwork prompts reflection on the availability of pharmaceutical solutions and highlights disparities in healthcare access globally.

[f] Description of Disease & Etiology

Aspirin is widely used to relieve pain, reduce inflammation, and prevent blood clots. The medication works by inhibiting enzymes involved in the production of inflammatory chemicals in the body. Its use spans a range of conditions, illustrating its significance in medical treatment.

[g] Pathology Signs/Signifiers of Illness

The formation of crystals, like those shown in this artwork, can mimic certain pathological conditions where abnormal crystal formations occur in the body, such as gout or kidney stones. These conditions highlight the importance of understanding crystal growth and formation in medical research.

[h] Treatment

Treatments involving aspirin include pain management and prevention of conditions like heart attacks and strokes through its antiplatelet effects. Its versatility in therapeutic applications underscores its importance in modern medicine.

[i] Social Determinants of Illness

Access to medications like aspirin is influenced by social determinants such as socioeconomic status, education, and healthcare infrastructure. The artwork serves to remind us of the broader implications of medication availability and its role in health equity.
