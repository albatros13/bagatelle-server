f. Description of Disease & Etiology

Proteus Syndrome is a rare genetic disorder characterized by overgrowth of skin, bones, muscles, fatty tissues, and blood vessels. The condition is caused by a mosaic mutation in the AKT1 gene, which affects cell growth and division. This mosaicism means that not all cells carry the mutation, leading to the sporadic and varied manifestations of the syndrome.

g. Pathology Signs/Signifiers of Illness

Symptoms include asymmetric overgrowth of limbs, the skull, and other tissues; skin overgrowths such as epidermal nevi; and an increased risk of tumors. The illustration captures typical disproportionate growth, particularly in the cranial and facial regions, which are classic signifiers of the illness.

h. Treatment

Treatment focuses on managing symptoms and complications. Due to its complexity and variability, Proteus Syndrome requires a multi-disciplinary approach including surgical interventions, orthopedic procedures, and supportive therapies. Genetic counseling and regular monitoring are essential components of managing the condition.

i. Social Determinants of Illness

Access to healthcare, social support systems, and educational resources are critical determinants in the management of Proteus Syndrome. Stigmatization and lack of resources can lead to delayed diagnosis and treatment, hence highlighting the importance of public awareness and medical accessibility.
