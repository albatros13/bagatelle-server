Maternidade 1981

[a] Artist/ Group/Tribe: Gilberto Gomes is a Brazilian artist whose work often delves into themes of maternity and the intimate bond between mother and child. He is part of a larger movement of contemporary artists in Brazil who explore both personal and cultural narratives through their unique visual styles.

[b] Historical and Socio-Cultural Context (time-frame): Created in 1981, "Maternidade" emerges from a period marked by significant social and political changes in Brazil. This era followed years of military dictatorship, and artists like Gomes played an essential role in capturing the human experience amidst the country’s journey towards democracy.

[c] Symbolism and/ or Iconography: The artwork prominently features the theme of maternity, symbolizing nourishment, protection, and the profound connection between mother and child. The mother's posture and expression convey a sense of tranquility and strength, emblematic of maternal care and devotion.

[d] Stylistic Significance (elements of art/ principles of design): Gomes employs a realistic style with soft, evocative use of color to highlight emotional depth. The contrast between light and shadow accentuates the textures of the skin and fabric, drawing attention to the intimate interaction between the subjects. The composition directs the viewer's gaze toward the connecting line between the mother and child.

[e] Social / Cultural Inequities: The artwork subtly addresses themes of social inequity by representing a universal scene of motherhood against a backdrop of systemic social challenges. It alludes to the struggles faced by women, especially in underrepresented communities, emphasizing resilience and the universality of motherhood amidst adversity.

[f] Description of Disease & Etiology: While the artwork primarily focuses on healthy maternity, it opens a dialogue about maternal health issues prevalent in socio-economically constrained environments. Conditions such as malnutrition, maternal mortality, and access to prenatal care are indirectly referenced through the cultural context.

[g] Pathology Signs/Signifiers of Illness: The painting does not depict explicit signs of illness, instead, it implicitly references the importance of healthcare and the risks faced by mothers without adequate medical support. The serene portrayal of motherhood challenges viewers to consider the underlying health narratives.

[h] Treatment: In the context of the artwork, treatment refers to the broader interventions necessary to improve maternal health, such as better healthcare access, education, and nutrition. Public health initiatives are crucial in addressing these disparities within communities depicted in Gomes's work.

[i] Social Determinants of Illness: The painting emphasizes the influence of social determinants, such as economic stability, healthcare access, and educational opportunities, on maternal and child health. These factors significantly impact health outcomes in the communities represented by the artwork.
