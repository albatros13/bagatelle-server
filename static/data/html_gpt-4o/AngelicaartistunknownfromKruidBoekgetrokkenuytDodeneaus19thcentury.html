Angelica from Kruid Boekgetrokkenuyt Dodeneaus

[a] Artist/ Group/Tribe

The artwork titled "Angelica" is an illustration from a herbarium commonly referred to as the "Kruid Boekgetrokkenuyt Dodeneaus." The artist remains unknown, which is not uncommon in historical botanical illustrations where the focus is on the accuracy of plant depiction rather than the artist's identity. These illustrations often served educational purposes, offering crucial details of plants for medicinal and academic study.

[b] Historical and Socio-Cultural Context (Time-frame)

Originating from the 19th century, this illustration reflects a time when botanical studies were burgeoning due to the Age of Enlightenment's emphasis on scientific exploration and classification. During this period, Europe was experiencing a significant transformation with advancements in science and a growing interest in natural history. Botanical illustrations such as these found their place in both academic settings and among the growing middle-class curiosity for scientific and natural world understanding.

[c] Symbolism and/or Iconography

Angelica plants, noted here, have held symbolic significance in various cultures. Traditionally, they are associated with protection and healing. Often seen as an herb with magical qualities, angelica was used for warding off evil and providing a sense of well-being. The illustration captures its roots and foliage, significant components used in traditional medicine, underlining its role in herbal healing practices.

[d] Stylistic Significance (Elements of Art/Principles of Design)

[e] Social / Cultural Inequities

While botanical studies were increasingly popular, access to such knowledge was predominantly reserved for the educated elite. Lower socio-economic classes had limited access to these resources, which were primarily housed in universities or private collections. Moreover, the role of women in botanical illustration, often uncredited, highlights wider societal inequities of the time.

[f] Description of Disease & Etiology

Angelica has been used historically in the treatment of various ailments, such as digestive disorders and respiratory issues. Although not associated with a specific disease, its properties were believed to aid in the treatment of colds, fevers, and infections due to its anti-inflammatory and immune-boosting capabilities.

[g] Pathology Signs/Signifiers of Illness

The inclusion of angelica in medicinal texts often pertains to its effectiveness in alleviating symptoms such as stomach cramps, coughs, and inflammations. The illustration might have served as a reference for those diagnosing or treating conditions to identify and utilize this herb effectively.

[h] Treatment

Historically, angelica was prepared in various forms including tinctures, teas, and poultices. Its roots particularly were harvested for oils believed to assist in digestive improvement and respiratory relief. Its inclusion in herbal medicine was part of a broader approach to holistic healing practices prevailing during the period.

[i] Social Determinants of Illness

Access to angelica and other herbal remedies was often determined by social class and geographical location. While rural communities might have had easier access to this plant, urban populations depended on apothecaries. The knowledge and accessibility of such plants played a crucial role in personal and community health outcomes.
