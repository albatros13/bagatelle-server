Giandomenico Tiepolo: The Tooth Puller

[a] Artist/ Group/Tribe

Giandomenico Tiepolo, born in Venice in 1727, was an Italian painter and printmaker. As the son of the renowned artist Giovanni Battista Tiepolo, he was exposed to art from an early age. Giandomenico is known for his vivid frescoes and drawings, often depicting scenes of daily life with a satirical touch.

[b] Historical and Socio-Cultural context (time-frame)

"The Tooth Puller" was painted in 1754, during a time when Venice was a vibrant center of commerce and culture. This period saw the flourishing of Rococo art, characterized by ornate details and expressive compositions. The depiction of a tooth pulling scene is indicative of the period's interest in satirical commentary on social issues.

[c] Symbolism and/ or iconography

In this work, the act of tooth pulling can be interpreted as a metaphor for the extraction of folly or ignorance. The bustling crowd around the tooth puller symbolizes society's sometimes blind attachment to quackery and superficial remedies. The expressions and gestures of the figures add a layer of humor and critique of contemporary medical practices.

[d] Stylistic Significance (elements of art/ principles of design)

The painting exemplifies Rococo elements through its dynamic composition, use of light and shadow, and vibrant colors. Tiepolo's skillful use of perspective and detail draws viewers into the scene, making them a part of the chaotic crowd. The contrast between the animated figures and the calm sky enhances the dynamic nature of the artwork.

[e] Social / Cultural Inequities

The artwork highlights social inequities by portraying the questionable medical practices of the time, often accessible mostly to the lower classes seeking affordable treatments. The depiction critiques how privilege and access to proper medical care were not evenly distributed across society.

[f] Description of Disease & Etiology

Toothaches during the 18th century could be attributed to poor dental hygiene, lack of knowledge about dental diseases, and nutritional deficiencies. The common causes included cavities, gum disease, and tooth abscesses, often exacerbated by the lack of preventive care and effective treatments.

[g] Pathology Signs/Signifiers of Illness

The figure undergoing tooth extraction may display signs typical of dental pathologies such as swollen cheeks, discomfort, and the distress associated with severe toothache. The presence of such symptoms points to the severity of the condition requiring extraction as a last resort.

[h] Treatment

During the 18th century, tooth extraction was a common remedy for dental ailments, performed by barbers or itinerant practitioners. These procedures were often crude and painful, lacking anesthesia or proper hygiene, highlighting the rudimentary state of dental care at that time.

[i] Social Determinants of Illness

Factors like socioeconomic status, access to nutritional food, and knowledge about personal hygiene played crucial roles in dental health. The reliance on unqualified medical personnel illustrated the impact of educational and resource inequities on public health outcomes.
