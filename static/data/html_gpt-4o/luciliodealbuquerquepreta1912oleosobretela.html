Art Historical and Biomedical Analysis

[a] Artist/ Group/Tribe

Lucílio de Albuquerque was a renowned Brazilian painter known for his depictions of everyday life and the nuances of light and shadow in his works. Emerging in the early 20th century, his art often explored themes related to societal and cultural issues, with a keen sensitivity to the human condition. Trained in Paris, his style drew influences from European techniques while retaining a distinct Brazilian character.

[b] Historical and Socio-Cultural Context (Time-Frame)

The painting "Pret a" created in 1912 exists in a period when Brazil was undergoing significant socio-cultural transformations. The abolition of slavery in 1888 had led to a shift in the societal structure, with former slaves and their descendants trying to find a place in a rapidly modernizing society. The early 20th century was a time of establishing national identity and redefining cultural boundaries in post-colonial Brazil.

[c] Symbolism and/or Iconography

The artwork depicts a Black mother nursing a child, symbolizing themes of motherhood, nurturing, and racial identity. The depiction of the subjects' skin tones, along with the rustic surroundings, highlights issues of race and class. The baby's contrasting lighter complexion might suggest themes of mixed heritage or commentary on racial dynamics in Brazilian society at the time.

[d] Stylistic Significance (Elements of Art/ Principles of Design)

Albuquerque employs a soft palette with gentle brushwork, focusing on the tender interaction between mother and child. The use of light accentuates the figures amidst the muted background, drawing the viewer's attention to the intimacy of the scene. The composition balances between realism and impressionism, capturing the essence of the moment rather than detailed realism.

[e] Social/Cultural Inequities

This piece underscores the racial inequities present in early 20th-century Brazil. The subject matter, a Black woman in a domestic, nurturing role, reflects the lingering effects of slavery and the socio-economic disparities faced by Afro-Brazilians. Such representation calls attention to the inequalities that continued to persist despite the legal abolishment of slavery.

[f] Description of Disease & Etiology

In the context of this analysis, the artwork does not depict a specific disease; however, it can be used to discuss historical health issues faced by Afro-Brazilian populations, such as malnutrition and infectious diseases, exacerbated by poverty and lack of access to healthcare.

[g] Pathology Signs/Signifiers of Illness

The painting does not explicitly signify illness, but the era depicted was rife with challenges that heightened disease susceptibility, including poor living conditions and inadequate nutrition which were common among marginalized communities.

[h] Treatment

Treatment options during early 20th century Brazil were limited and often inaccessible to poorer populations, prominently including indigenous and Afro-Brazilian groups. Traditional remedies played a significant role, alongside emerging medical practices initiated by public health movements aiming to improve living conditions.

[i] Social Determinants of Illness

Key social determinants affecting health in this context include poverty, racial discrimination, lack of education, and inadequate access to healthcare services. These factors collectively contributed to the prevalence of disease and hindered overall well-being in Afro-Brazilian communities.
