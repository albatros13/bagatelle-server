Art Historical and Biomedical Analysis

[a] Artist/ Group/Tribe

The photograph was produced in the studio of Charles Eisenmann, a notable photographer based in New York City during the latter half of the 19th century. Eisenmann was known for his portraits of sideshow performers and circus attractions, capturing the unique individuals who often existed on the fringes of Victorian society.

[b] Historical and Socio-Cultural context (time-frame)

The photograph dates back to approximately 1880, a period marked by the height of the Gilded Age in America. This era was characterized by rapid industrial growth, urban expansion, and increasing fascination with curiosities and oddities. Photography in this time served both as a form of entertainment and documentation, and Eisenmann’s studio became a hub for capturing the extraordinary individuals who performed in traveling shows.

[c] Symbolism and/ or iconography

The imagery in Eisenmann’s photographs often reflected a society intrigued by difference, yet also one that commodified and exploited it. The giant figure and the regular-sized figure in military attire juxtaposed with each other emphasize notions of power, spectacle, and otherness. This duality worked as both a literal and metaphorical depiction of Victorian societal structures.

[d] Stylistic Significance (elements of art/ principles of design)

Eisenmann’s work utilized the formal elements of line, form, and contrast. The positioning and posture of the subjects exude a powerful vertical line, emphasizing stature and presence. The sepia tone common in his portraits added a sense of age and authenticity while contrasting light and shadow accentuated the unique physical characteristics of the subjects.

[e] Social / Cultural Inequities

The subjects of Eisenmann’s photography, often individuals with physical differences or disabilities, were frequently objectified for public entertainment in sideshow exhibitions. This highlights a period of cultural insensitivity and underlines social inequities where such individuals were marginalized but also sensationalized.

[f] Description of Disease & Etiology

The likely medical condition associated with the larger figure might be gigantism or acromegaly, disorders caused by an excess of growth hormone. Gigantism occurs when the condition manifests in children or adolescents, leading to a generalized increase in body size with particularly pronounced limb growth.

[g] Pathology Signs/Signifiers of Illness

Signs of gigantism include abnormal growth spurts, particularly in height, and disproportionate body features. In portraits capturing such subjects, noticeable indicators might include elongated limbs, enlarged hands and feet, and sometimes distinct facial features.

[h] Treatment

During the late 19th century, awareness and medical intervention for conditions like gigantism were limited. Treatment, where possible, involved addressing hormonal imbalances, but effective medical therapies such as surgery or medication significantly lagged behind compared to modern advancements.

[i] Social Determinants of Illness

Factors such as access to healthcare, socioeconomic background, and societal attitudes towards physical abnormalities played crucial roles in the experiences of individuals with gigantism. Limited understanding and fear often resulted in ostracism or exploitation, with few opportunities outside entertainment lines such as circuses.
